    Parts and nesting¶

    When to nest¶

    • Multiple small parts from the same material
    • Parts that fit conveniently next to or inside each other
    • To reduce waste and production cost
    • Nesting is optional — uploading parts separately with quantity also gives automatic discounts

    Spacing requirements¶

    Material Minimum gap
    Standard 2 mm
    Foam 4 mm

    Insufficient spacing causes burn marks and smoke deposits on parts.

    Shared cutting lines¶

    Parts can share a single cut line along one edge to reduce total cutting length and cost.

    Rules for shared lines:

    • Works best for rectangular shapes placed edge-to-edge
    • Remove duplicate lines where shapes connect
    • Do NOT use shared lines for:
      • Small or precision parts (accuracy not guaranteed)
      • Parts with rounded corners
      • Parts requiring dimensional accuracy on the shared edge

    Creating a nest¶

    1. Set artboard to maximum material dimensions
    2. Arrange parts with proper spacing (see above)
    3. Follow standard drawing rules for layer names and line colors (see specs)
    4. Remove duplicate lines at shared edges

    Nesting software¶

    • RhinoNest (Rhinoceros plugin) for complex layouts
